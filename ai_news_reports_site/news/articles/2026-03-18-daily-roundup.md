## ## Middle East Turmoil and Global Repercussions

### Iran's Intelligence Minister Assassinated
Iran's intelligence minister, Esmail Khatib, was killed in an Israeli airstrike, officially confirmed by Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian. This assassination marks a critical escalation in the ongoing conflict between Iran and Israel, with Iranian military officials promising "decisive action" in retaliation. The killing has heightened fears of broader military engagements in the region.

### Rising Oil Prices Amid Military Conflict
As tensions escalate, oil prices have surged, nearing $110 per barrel. The conflict has already led to significant disruptions in energy supply, prompting U.S. lawmakers to call for a windfall tax on major oil companies. This backlash reflects rising public anger over potential profiteering amid a humanitarian crisis. The Federal Reserve is now grappling with the inflationary pressures caused by these soaring oil prices, deciding to keep interest rates steady despite concerns about the weakening U.S. job market.

The rise in oil prices is particularly alarming for consumers, who are already feeling the pinch at the pump. With Trump waiving shipping laws to lower oil costs, the administration's efforts to stabilize prices may only provide temporary relief. 

### Israeli Military Actions in Lebanon
In another significant development, Israel has destroyed river bridges in southern Lebanon, leading to over 968 casualties since the beginning of March, including more than 100 children. This ongoing violence raises humanitarian concerns and could provoke further retaliatory actions from Lebanon, exacerbating tensions throughout the region.
